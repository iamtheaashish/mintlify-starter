### 2026

1. [Why ATMs didn’t kill bank teller jobs, but the iPhone did](https://open.substack.com/pub/davidoks/p/why-the-atm-didnt-kill-bank-teller?utm_campaign=post-expanded-share&utm_medium=web) by David Oks

    Probably one of the best blogs I've read on whether or not AI will replace humans and cause job losses. 

2. [Poker, Not Chess: Make Better Decisions in an Uncertain World](https://open.substack.com/pub/speculator0/p/poker-not-chess-how-to-think-clearly?utm_campaign=post-expanded-share&utm_medium=web) by Zero Repository

    A random person was kind enough to like my tweet (which I use to vent out my frustration). And then I checked his X profile and found out that he has a Substack. Went there and found out this masterpiece. Whatever he has written is absolute truth because it is backed by math. This is what I liked the most about this article: John Von Neumann, the father of game theory and inventor of the architecture behind modern computers, saw poker, not chess, as the ideal metaphor for decision-making in life.

3. [Better Democracy Without Elections](https://open.substack.com/pub/wahal/p/elections?utm_campaign=post-expanded-share&utm_medium=web) by Mrinal Wahal

    Technology could enable more precise governance and smart collective choice. This article comes from a competent engineer and not just a political theorist. Implementing this model has challenges for me. My knowledge with blockchain is limited. Mrinal told me to get started with Bitcoin paper.
